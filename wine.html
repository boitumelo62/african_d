Swartland Region

A LEGACY OF EXCELLENCE

The Swartland region, with its rich history of viticulture, is renowned for its ability to produce high-quality grapes. One of the distinctive features of this region is its dryland bush vine vineyards, which thrive in the clay soils.

Unlike trellised vines that require significant irrigation, bush vines are more drought-resistant and grow closer to the ground, benefiting from the natural contours of the land. This method of cultivation promotes deeper root systems, allowing the vines to access water and nutrients more effectively, which enhances the resilience and quality of the grapes.

The minimal need for irrigation in the Swartland, due to the excellent moisture retention in the soil, allows the vines to grow naturally and sustainably. This practice not only conserves water but also encourages the vines to produce grapes with more concentrated flavors and complexity.

The philosophy of minimal intervention is central to the Swartland ethos, where winemakers strive to let the grapes express their true character. This approach results in wines that are a genuine reflection of their terroir, with a depth and authenticity that can be tasted in every bottle.

Furthermore, the use of older barrels is a common practice in the Swartland region. These barrels provide structure and subtle oak influence without overpowering the fruit, allowing the quality of the viticulture to shine through. The focus on viticulture is paramount, as the region believes that great wine starts in the vineyard.

By emphasizing the natural strengths of the land and the grapes, Swartland winemakers create wines that are both complex and true to their origins.

Grenache Noir

Sourced from the rare, dryland bush vine vineyards, our Grenache Noir exemplifies the intense concentration of flavors that these resilient, low-yield vines produce.

Bush vines yield grapes rich in fruit and complexity, nurtured by the clay soils of the Swartland region, which provide excellent moisture retention, minimizing the need for irrigation and allowing the vines to flourish naturally. The result is a wine that is vibrant and richly textured, with layers of fruit and a beautifully balanced structure.
